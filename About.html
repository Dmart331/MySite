What is Augmented Reality? May 21, 2018

Most people ask me "What is AR" when I tell them that I am an AR Developer. The next question, most of the time, is "So is that like VR?". This is a common question because most people have never been able to experience both of the technologies. The main difference between the two is, as a wise man once said, "AR brings things to you, while VR takes you there ". In other words. AR can bring something into your world, it can augment your surrounding reality. VR is different as it completely replaces your reality with a new one. Both are extremely exciting and offer solotions to problems that we didn't even know we had before. Below are some examples of projects that I was able to work on

Object Recognition

In the above example, you can see that with Augmented Reality we can take an object that most people aren't used to working with and highlighting different important areas - making it easier for the user to use the product. The first time I put a computer together I looked at the motherboard and was instantly intemedated. I thought it would be helpful if you could point your phone at the board and highlight the parts that I needed to worry about. The above example is my proof of concept. The user is able to click the highlighted components and get more information on them.

Target Based Augmented Reality

Target based AR works simalarly to object based recognition. The computer is looking for specfic tracking points on an image you have provided. This image can literally be for anything - making target based AR extremely valuable for marketing. In the above example, the targets I chose are for a steampunk theme. Each target is a different part of a mechanical item needed to figure out a puzzle.
